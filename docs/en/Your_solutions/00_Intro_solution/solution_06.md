## 6. Function Analysis

Consider the function:

$$
f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7
$$

We want to determine whether the function has a **local maximum or minimum**, and find its value.

---

## Step 1: Identify the Type of Function

The function is a **quadratic function** of the form:

$$
f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c
$$

where:

- \(a = 3\)
- \(b = -12\)
- \(c = 7\)

Because \(a > 0\), the parabola **opens upward**, which means the function has a **local minimum**.

---

## Step 2: Find the Vertex (Critical Point)

The \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex is given by:

x = -\frac{b}{2a}

Substitute the values:

$$
x = -\frac{-12}{2(3)}
$$

$$
x = \frac{12}{6}
$$

$$
x = 2
$$

---

## Step 3: Find the Minimum Value

Now evaluate the function at \(x = 2\):

$$
f(2) = 3(2)^2 - 12(2) + 7
$$

Compute each term:

$$
3(4) - 24 + 7
$$

$$
12 - 24 + 7
$$

$$
-5
$$

---

## Step 4: Identify the Extremum

The vertex of the parabola is:

$$
(2, -5)
$$

Since the parabola opens upward, this point represents a **local minimum**.

---

## Final Answer

Local minimum occurs at:

$$
x = 2
$$

Minimum value:

$$
f(2) = -5
$$

Vertex of the function:

$$
(2, -5)
$$
