## 7. Logic & Series

A bicycle is \(10\) meters from a wall and moves toward it at a constant speed of \(1 \text{ m/s}\). A fly starts from the bicycle's front wheel and flies toward the wall at \(2 \text{ m/s}\). Each time it hits the wall, it instantly turns around and flies back to the bicycle, repeating this motion until the bicycle reaches the wall.

We want to find the **total distance** the fly travels before being crushed.

---

## Step 1: Find How Long the Bicycle Takes to Reach the Wall

The bicycle starts \(10\) meters from the wall and moves at \(1 \text{ m/s}\).

Using:

$$
\text{time} = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{speed}}
$$

we get:

$$
t = \frac{10}{1} = 10 \text{ s}
$$

So, the bicycle reaches the wall in:

$$
10 \text{ seconds}
$$

---

## Step 2: Determine the Fly's Speed and Travel Time

The fly keeps moving continuously until the bicycle reaches the wall.

Its speed is constant:

$$
2 \text{ m/s}
$$

And it flies for the same total time:

$$
10 \text{ s}
$$

---

## Step 3: Compute the Total Distance Traveled by the Fly

Using:

$$
\text{distance} = \text{speed} \times \text{time}
$$

we get:

$$
d = 2 \times 10
$$

$$
d = 20
$$

---

## Explanation

Although the fly makes many back-and-forth trips, we do **not** need to add each trip separately. The key idea is that the fly is always in motion for the entire time before the bicycle reaches the wall.

Since:

- the bicycle takes \(10\) seconds to reach the wall
- the fly moves at \(2 \text{ m/s}\) for all \(10\) seconds

the fly's total distance is simply:

$$
20 \text{ meters}
$$

---

## Final Answer

The total distance traveled by the fly before being crushed is:

$$
20 \text{ meters}
$$
