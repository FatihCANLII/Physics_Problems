## 8. Definite Integrals

We are asked to calculate the **area under the curve** of the function:

$$
f(x) = \sin(x)
$$

from:

$$
x = 0 \quad \text{to} \quad x = \pi
$$

---

## Step 1: Write the Definite Integral

The area under a curve between two points is given by the definite integral:

$$
\int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x)\,dx
$$

---

## Step 2: Find the Antiderivative

The antiderivative of \(\sin(x)\) is:

$$
-\cos(x)
$$

So the integral becomes:

$$
[-\cos(x)]_{0}^{\pi}
$$

---

## Step 3: Evaluate at the Bounds

Substitute the upper and lower limits:

$$
-\cos(\pi) - (-\cos(0))
$$

---

## Step 4: Use Known Cosine Values

\[
\cos(\pi) = -1
\]

\[
\cos(0) = 1
\]

Substitute:

$$
-(-1) - (-1)
$$

$$
1 + 1
$$

$$
2
$$

---

## Final Answer

The total area under the curve is:

$$
2
$$
