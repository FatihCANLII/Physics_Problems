## 5. Trigonometry

A vector $\vec{A}$ has a magnitude of $15$ and makes an angle of $\theta = 60^\circ$ with the horizontal axis. We want to calculate its **horizontal** and **vertical** components.

---

## Step 1: Component Formulas

For a vector with magnitude $A$ and angle $\theta$:

Horizontal component:

$$
A_x = A \cos(\theta)
$$

Vertical component:

$$
A_y = A \sin(\theta)
$$

---

## Step 2: Substitute the Given Values

Magnitude:

$$
A = 15
$$

Angle:

$$
\theta = 60^\circ
$$

So the components become:

$$
A_x = 15\cos(60^\circ)
$$

$$
A_y = 15\sin(60^\circ)
$$

---

## Step 3: Use Known Trigonometric Values

$$
\cos(60^\circ) = \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

Substitute these values:

$$
A_x = 15 \times \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
A_y = 15 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

---

## Step 4: Calculate the Components

Horizontal component:

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

Vertical component:

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

Approximate value:

$$
A_y \approx 12.99
$$

---

## Final Answer

Horizontal component:

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

Vertical component:

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99
$$
