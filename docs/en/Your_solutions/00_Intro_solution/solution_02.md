## 2. Systems of Equations

We need to solve the system:

\[
2x + 3y = 12
\]

\[
x - y = 1
\]

We want values of \(x\) and \(y\) that satisfy **both** equations at the same time.

---

## Step 1: Solve one equation for one variable

From the second equation:

\[
x - y = 1
\]

Solve for \(x\):

\[
x = y + 1
\]

---

## Step 2: Substitute into the first equation

Now substitute \(x = y + 1\) into:

\[
2x + 3y = 12
\]

This gives:

\[
2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
\]

Expand:

\[
2y + 2 + 3y = 12
\]

Combine like terms:

\[
5y + 2 = 12
\]

Subtract 2 from both sides:

\[
5y = 10
\]

Divide by 5:

\[
y = 2
\]

---

## Step 3: Find \(x\)

Use:

\[
x = y + 1
\]

Substitute \(y = 2\):

\[
x = 2 + 1
\]

\[
x = 3
\]

---

## Step 4: Check the solution

Check in the first equation:

\[
2x + 3y = 2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12
\]

Check in the second equation:

\[
x - y = 3 - 2 = 1
\]

Both equations are satisfied.

---

## Final Answer

\[
x = 3 \quad y = 2
\]
