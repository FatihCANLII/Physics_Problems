# 5. Percentage Calculation

We use the concept of **Percentage Uncertainty** to show how precise a measurement is. The time measurement given in the question is as follows:

$$t = 5.45 \pm 0.22 \text{ seconds}$$

In this file, we will calculate the percentage uncertainty of this measurement in 3 simple steps.

---

## 1. Identify the Given Values

Before moving to the formula, let's clarify the data we have:

* **Measured Value ($t$):** $5.45 \text{ s}$ (The central value of our measurement)
* **Absolute Uncertainty ($\Delta t$):** $0.22 \text{ s}$ (The margin of error or uncertainty in the measurement)

---

## 2. The Formula to Use

Percentage uncertainty is found by dividing the absolute uncertainty by the measured value and multiplying by one hundred percent ($100\%$):

> $$\text{Percentage Uncertainty} = \left( \frac{\Delta t}{t} \right) \times 100\%$$

*The core logic here is to answer the question: "What percentage of the whole ($5.45$) does the error ($0.22$) represent?"*

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Solution

### **Step 1: Substitute Values into the Formula**
We start the process by placing our numbers into the formula:

$$\text{Percentage Uncertainty} = \left( \frac{0.22}{5.45} \right) \times 100\%$$

### **Step 2: Calculate the Ratio (Division)**
Let's perform the division inside the parentheses:

$$\frac{0.22}{5.45} \approx 0.040367$$

### **Step 3: Convert to a Percentage**
Multiply this decimal number by $100$ and add the percentage symbol:

$$0.040367 \times 100 \approx 4.0367\%$$

---

## Summary and Conclusion

In physics and chemistry reports, uncertainty values are usually rounded according to significant figures.

* **Calculated Value:** $4.0367\%$
* **Rounded Presentation Result:** **$4.04\%$** (or approximately **$4\%$** for a simpler explanation)

> **Presentation Tip:** You can conclude your slide by mentioning to your audience that this measurement has a quite low error margin of about $4\%$, making the time measurement highly reliable.
