# 4. Relative Uncertainty

The speedometer reads:

$$
v = 60\text{ km/h}
$$

The uncertainty is:

$$
5\%
$$

The absolute uncertainty is:

$$
\Delta v = 60 \times \frac{5}{100}
$$

$$
\Delta v = 3\text{ km/h}
$$

So the actual speed can be between:

$$
60 - 3 = 57\text{ km/h}
$$

and

$$
60 + 3 = 63\text{ km/h}
$$

Final answer:

$$
\boxed{57\text{ km/h} \leq v \leq 63\text{ km/h}}
$$
