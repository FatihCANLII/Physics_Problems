# 1. Propagation of Error I

In this question, the radius of a sphere is given with an uncertainty:

$$
r = (6.20 \pm 0.05)\text{ cm}
$$

The volume of a sphere is calculated using the formula:

$$
V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3
$$

Now we substitute the measured radius into the formula:

$$
V = \frac{4}{3}\pi(6.20)^3
$$

$$
V = 998.31\text{ cm}^3
$$

So, the volume of the sphere is approximately:

$$
V \approx 998\text{ cm}^3
$$

Next, we calculate the uncertainty in the volume. Since volume depends on \(r^3\), a small uncertainty in radius affects the volume according to:

$$
\Delta V = 4\pi r^2 \Delta r
$$

Substituting the values:

$$
\Delta V = 4\pi(6.20)^2(0.05)
$$

$$
\Delta V = 24.15\text{ cm}^3
$$

Therefore, the uncertainty is approximately:

$$
\Delta V \approx 24\text{ cm}^3
$$

Final answer:

$$
\boxed{V = (998 \pm 24)\text{ cm}^3}
$$
