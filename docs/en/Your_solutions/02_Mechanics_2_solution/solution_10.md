## 10. Force Field and Power

---

## 🔹 Given

- Mass: m = 0.5 kg  

Position:

x(t) = 5t² − t  
y(t) = 2t³  
z(t) = −3t + 2  

---

## 🟢 Part A: Velocity

Velocity = derivative of position

vx = dx/dt = 10t − 1  
vy = dy/dt = 6t²  
vz = dz/dt = −3  

---

### ✅ Result:

v(t) = (10t − 1, 6t², −3)

---

## 🟢 Part B: Momentum

p = m v  

px = 0.5(10t − 1) = 5t − 0.5  
py = 0.5(6t²) = 3t²  
pz = 0.5(−3) = −1.5  

---

### ✅ Result:

p(t) = (5t − 0.5, 3t², −1.5)

---

## 🟢 Part C: Acceleration

Acceleration = derivative of velocity

ax = d(10t − 1)/dt = 10  
ay = d(6t²)/dt = 12t  
az = d(−3)/dt = 0  

---

### ✅ Result:

a(t) = (10, 12t, 0)

---

## 🟢 Part D: Force

F = m a  

Fx = 0.5 × 10 = 5  
Fy = 0.5 × 12t = 6t  
Fz = 0  

---

### ✅ Result:

F(t) = (5, 6t, 0)

---

## 🟢 Part E: Power

Power = F · v (dot product)

P = Fx·vx + Fy·vy + Fz·vz  

---

### Step 1: Substitute

P = 5(10t − 1) + (6t)(6t²) + 0  

---

### Step 2: Simplify

P = 50t − 5 + 36t³  

---

### ✅ Result:

P(t) = 36t³ + 50t − 5  

---

## 📌 Summary

| Quantity | Expression |
|----------|----------|
| Velocity | (10t − 1, 6t², −3) |
| Momentum | (5t − 0.5, 3t², −1.5) |
| Acceleration | (10, 12t, 0) |
| Force | (5, 6t, 0) |
| Power | 36t³ + 50t − 5 |
