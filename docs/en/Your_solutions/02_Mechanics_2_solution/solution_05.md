## 5. Inelastic Collision

---

## 🔹 Given

- m1 = 70 kg (runner)  
- v1 = 3 m/s  
- m2 = 140 kg (cart)  
- v2 = 0 m/s  

---

## 🟢 Part A: Final Speed

### Step 1: Use Momentum Conservation

m1·v1 + m2·v2 = (m1 + m2)·vf  

Since the cart is at rest:

m1·v1 = (m1 + m2)·vf  

---

### Step 2: Solve for vf

vf = (m1 × v1) / (m1 + m2)

vf = (70 × 3) / (70 + 140)  
vf = 210 / 210  

vf = **1.0 m/s**

---

### ✅ Answer:
**Final speed = 1.0 m/s**

---

## 🟢 Part B: Is Kinetic Energy Conserved?

### Step 1: Initial kinetic energy

KE_initial = (1/2) m1 v1²  
KE_initial = 0.5 × 70 × 3²  
KE_initial = 0.5 × 70 × 9  
KE_initial = **315 J**

---

### Step 2: Final kinetic energy

KE_final = (1/2) (m1 + m2) vf²  
KE_final = 0.5 × 210 × (1.0)²  
KE_final = **105 J**

---

### Step 3: Compare

- Initial KE = 315 J  
- Final KE = 105 J  

Energy decreased → not conserved

---

### ✅ Final Answer:
- **Kinetic energy is NOT conserved**  
- Some energy is transformed into heat, sound, and deformation
