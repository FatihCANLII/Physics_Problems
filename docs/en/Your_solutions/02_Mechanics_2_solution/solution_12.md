## 12. Work and Energy with a Constant Force

---

## 🔹 Given

- Mass: m = 2 kg  
- Force: F = (6, 2) N  

Initial conditions:
- v(0) = (1, −1) m/s  
- r(0) = (0, 0) m  

---

## 🟢 Part A: Acceleration

a = F / m  

ax = 6 / 2 = 3  
ay = 2 / 2 = 1  

---

### ✅ Result:

a(t) = (3, 1)  (constant)

---

## 🟢 Part B: Velocity

v(t) = v0 + a t  

vx = 1 + 3t  
vy = −1 + t  

---

### ✅ Result:

v(t) = (1 + 3t, −1 + t)

---

## 🟢 Part C: Position

r(t) = r0 + v0 t + (1/2) a t²  

---

### Components:

x(t) = 0 + 1·t + (1/2)(3)t²  
x(t) = t + (3/2)t²  

y(t) = 0 − t + (1/2)(1)t²  
y(t) = −t + (1/2)t²  

---

### ✅ Result:

r(t) = (t + 1.5t², −t + 0.5t²)

---

## 🟢 Part D: Trajectory

Eliminate time:

From x(t):

t + 1.5t²  

From y(t):

−t + 0.5t²  

---

### Interpretation:

- Motion is **parabolic**
- Similar to projectile motion with constant acceleration

---

## 🟢 Part E: Work at t = 3 s

Work = F · displacement  

---

### Step 1: Position at t = 3

x(3) = 3 + 1.5(9) = 3 + 13.5 = 16.5  
y(3) = −3 + 0.5(9) = −3 + 4.5 = 1.5  

Displacement = (16.5, 1.5)

---

### Step 2: Work

W = (6, 2) · (16.5, 1.5)  

W = 6×16.5 + 2×1.5  
W = 99 + 3  

W = **102 J**

---

### ✅ Result:
**Work = 102 J**

---

## 🟢 Part F: Work-Energy Theorem

### Step 1: Initial KE

KE_initial = (1/2)m(vx² + vy²)  

= 0.5 × 2 × (1² + (−1)²)  
= 1 × (1 + 1) = 2 J  

---

### Step 2: Final KE (t = 3)

vx = 1 + 9 = 10  
vy = −1 + 3 = 2  

KE_final = (1/2) × 2 × (10² + 2²)  
= 1 × (100 + 4) = 104 J  

---

### Step 3: Compare

ΔKE = 104 − 2 = **102 J**

---

### ✅ Verified:
Work = ΔKE → **102 J = 102 J ✔**

---
