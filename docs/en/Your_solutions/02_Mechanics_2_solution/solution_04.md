## 4. Energy & Momentum

---

## 🔹 Given

- m₁ = 0.5 kg (moving block)  
- m₂ = 1.5 kg (at rest)  
- Height: h = 3.0 m  
- g = 9.8 m/s²  

---

## 🟢 Step 1: Speed of First Block at Bottom

Use energy conservation:

mgh = (1/2)mv²  

Mass cancels:

v₁ = √(2gh)

---

### Calculate:

v₁ = √(2 × 9.8 × 3.0)  
v₁ = √58.8  
v₁ ≈ **7.67 m/s**

---

## 🟢 Step 2: Use Momentum Conservation (Perfectly Inelastic Collision)

m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = (m₁ + m₂)v_f  

Since second block is at rest:

m₁v₁ = (m₁ + m₂)v_f  

---

### Solve for final speed:

vf = (m1 × v1) / (m1 + m2)

vf = (0.5 × 7.67) / (0.5 + 1.5)  
vf = 3.835 / 2  

vf ≈ **1.92 m/s**

---

## ✅ Final Answer:
**Speed after collision ≈ 1.92 m/s**

---
