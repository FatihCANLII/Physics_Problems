# 4. Energy & Momentum

## 🧩 Part 1: Speed of the First Block

The block slides down, so its **potential energy** converts into **kinetic energy**.

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Mass cancels:

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 3.0}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{58.8}
$$

$$
v \approx 7.67 \, m/s
$$

👉 This is the speed of the first block just before the collision.

---

## 🧩 Part 2: Collision (Perfectly Inelastic)

The two blocks **stick together** after colliding.

That means:
- **Momentum is conserved**
- **Mechanical energy is not conserved**

---

## 📌 Formula

$$
m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Since the second block is initially at rest:

$$
v_2 = 0
$$

So the equation becomes:

$$
m_1v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

---

## 🧮 Step 3: Solve for Final Speed

Given:
- $m_1 = 0.5 \, kg$
- $m_2 = 1.5 \, kg$
- $v_1 = 7.67 \, m/s$

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v_f = \frac{0.5 \times 7.67}{0.5 + 1.5}
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2}
$$

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \, m/s
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

**Speed after the collision ≈ 1.92 m/s**
