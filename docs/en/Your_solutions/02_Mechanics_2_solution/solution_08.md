## 8. Work of a Variable Force

---

## 🔹 Given

F(x) = −k x  

---

## 🟢 Part A: Equation of Motion

### Step 1: Use Newton’s Second Law

F = m a  

m d²x/dt² = −k x  

---

### Step 2: Rearrange

d²x/dt² + (k/m) x = 0  

---

### Step 3: Solution (SHM)

x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ)  

where:

ω = √(k / m)

---

### ✅ Result:
The motion is **simple harmonic motion (SHM)**

---

## 🟢 Part B: Work Done (0 → x₀)

Work = ∫ F(x) dx  

W = ∫(0 → x₀) (−k x) dx  

---

### Step 1: Integrate

W = −k ∫ x dx  
W = −k (x² / 2)  

---

### Step 2: Apply limits

W = −(1/2) k x₀²  

---

### ✅ Result:
**Work done = −(1/2) k x₀²**

---

## 🟢 Part C: Potential Energy

Potential energy is defined as:

U(x) = −W  

---

### So:

U(x) = (1/2) k x²  

---

### ✅ Result:
**U(x) = (1/2) k x²**

---

## 🟢 Part D: Verify Relationship

Check:

F = − dU/dx  

---

### Step 1: Differentiate

dU/dx = k x  

---

### Step 2: Apply minus sign

F = −k x  

---

### ✅ Verified:
**F = −dU/dx is correct**

---

## 🟢 Part E: Graphs

### Force F(x):
- Straight line through origin  
- Negative slope (−k)  
- Decreases linearly with x  

---

### Potential Energy U(x):
- Upward-opening parabola  
- Minimum at x = 0  
- Always positive  

---

### ✅ Interpretation:
- System behaves like a **spring**
- Energy stored as elastic potential energy
- Force always pulls toward equilibrium

---
