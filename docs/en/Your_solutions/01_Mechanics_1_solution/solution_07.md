Given:
x(t) = 2t²  
y(t) = 3t³  

---

## Step 1: Eliminate parameter t

From x(t):
x = 2t² ⇒ t² = x/2 ⇒ t = √(x/2)

Substitute into y:

y = 3t³ = 3(t)(t²)
        = 3(√(x/2))(x/2)

So:

y = (3x/2)√(x/2)

---

## Step 2: Trajectory

The path is a **curved (nonlinear) increasing curve** in the first quadrant, starting at (0,0), with y growing faster than x.

---

## Step 3: Velocity vector

v(t) = dr/dt

x'(t) = 4t  
y'(t) = 9t²  

So:

v(t) = (4t)i + (9t²)j

---

## Step 4: Speed (magnitude of velocity)

|v(t)| = √[(4t)² + (9t²)²]  
       = √(16t² + 81t⁴)  
       = t√(16 + 81t²)

---

## Step 5: Acceleration vector

a(t) = dv/dt

x''(t) = 4  
y''(t) = 18t  

So:

a(t) = 4i + (18t)j

---

## Step 6: Magnitude of acceleration

|a(t)| = √[4² + (18t)²]  
       = √(16 + 324t²)

---

## Step 7: Is acceleration constant?

No.

Because the j-component (18t) depends on time, the acceleration vector changes with t.

---

## Final Answer

Trajectory: y = (3x/2)√(x/2)  

v(t) = (4t)i + (9t²)j
